USDA Food Nutrition Data Examination

Introduction:
The general public is becoming more health conscious as there is an increase awareness of the the nutritional values from the food that we consume on a daily basis. Some are trying to develop different ways in making eating efficient by promoting healthful nutrition or reducing the consumption of unhealthy foods. This examination will allow us to understand what foods are objectively healthful for us to eat by understanding the nutritional content.

Our Data Set:
We were given information on 4345 types of common food items by the United States department of Agriculture. Each of these items is described by its name and quantitative variables and qualitative variables to describe the item. Energy describes the amount of food energy in 100 gram of a food item in kcals. Protein, lipid, calcium, potassium, and sodium describes the amount of each nutrient the food contains in 100 gram samples. On the other hand, sugar is described in 10 categories where 1 represents 0-10 grams of sugar per 100 gram of food and so on. Similarly, water is described in 4 categories where 1 represents 0-25 grams of water per 100 grams of food and so on. Finally, fiber and cholesterol are represented with binary 0 or 1 whether there is more or less than 1 gram of fiber or 1 milligram of cholesterol on 100 grams of food. In this study, we are particularly interested in looking at the content of each nutrient and comparing that with energy to find which food is the most efficient for someone to gain the desired nutrient.

We will specifically look at the relationships between cholesterol and energy, cholesterol and lipid, cholesterol and protein, and protein and energy as these nutritions are what most people concerned with in regards to constructing a healthful diet.

Null Hypothesis: There is a relationship as the sample mean of there not being cholesterol is equal to the sample mean of there being cholesterol in regards to lipids.

Alternative Hypothesis: There is no relationship as the sample mean of there not being cholesterol is not equal to the sample mean of there being cholesterol in regards to lipids.

As described before, there are 2129 foods with 0 milligram of cholesterol. That mean in regards to lipids is 8.712 grams/100 grams with standard deviation of 20.301 grams/100 grams. There are 2216 foods with more than 0 milligram/100 grams of cholesterol, their mean in regards to lipids is 12.306 grams/100 grams with standard deviation of 12.874 grams/100 grams. Assuming that a = 0.10, we will calculate the confidence interval of the mean difference between the two groups:

(8.712 - 12.874) +/- 1.645 x (20.3012/2129 + 12.8742/2216)1/2 = (-5.014, -3.310)

We are then 90% confident that the mean difference between the two groups is between -5.014 and -3.310 and because 0 is not included in the confidence interval, we reject the null hypothesis then conclude that there is a relationship between cholesterol presence and average lipid content.

Assuming a = 0.05, our hypothesis:
